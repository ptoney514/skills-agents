# Example: Open Enrollment Communication Optimization

This example demonstrates how Lyra optimizes employee communication prompts for clarity and impact.

## Original Request (Vague)
```
Write an email about open enrollment
```

## Lyra's Analysis

### DECONSTRUCT
- **Core Intent:** Communicate open enrollment information to employees
- **Key Entities:** Email, open enrollment, benefits
- **Missing Context:**
  - What's changing this year?
  - Who is the audience (all employees? specific groups?)?
  - What action do employees need to take?
  - What are key dates and deadlines?
  - What tone fits the organizational culture?

### DIAGNOSE
- **Clarity Issues:** No purpose beyond general announcement
- **Specificity Gaps:** No changes noted, no deadlines, no call-to-action
- **Complexity Level:** BASIC mode sufficient (straightforward communication)
- **Audience Needs:** Employees need clarity on what's changing, what to do, and by when

### DEVELOP
**Techniques to Apply:**
- Role Assignment (Benefits communications specialist)
- Context Layering (Organization, changes, deadlines)
- Audience Calibration (Employee-friendly, action-oriented)
- Message Structuring (Key messages hierarchy)
- Tone Setting (Professional but accessible)

## Optimized Prompt

```
You are a benefits communications specialist drafting an open enrollment 
announcement email for Creighton University employees.

PURPOSE:
Inform employees about the upcoming benefits open enrollment period, 
highlight key changes, and drive timely enrollment completion.

CONTEXT:
- Organization: Creighton University (higher education, Catholic Jesuit values)
- Audience: All benefits-eligible employees (faculty and staff)
- Enrollment Period: November 1-15, 2024
- Key Changes This Year:
  * New mental health benefits through Lyra Health
  * HSA contribution limits increased to $4,150 (individual) / $8,300 (family)
  * New voluntary benefits: Pet insurance, Legal services
  * No changes to medical/dental/vision plan options
- Current Situation: First fully remote open enrollment (no paper forms)

KEY MESSAGES (in priority order):
1. Open enrollment runs November 1-15 (must enroll by 11:59pm on Nov 15)
2. Action required even if keeping same coverage (system reset)
3. Exciting new mental health benefit (Lyra Health)
4. New voluntary benefits available (pet insurance, legal services)
5. Virtual benefits fair on November 5 at 2pm (Teams link)
6. HR support available via drop-in hours and email

REQUIRED ELEMENTS:
- Subject line that captures urgency and benefit
- Opening that grabs attention (consider starting with new mental health benefit)
- Clear action items (what employees must do)
- Prominent deadline (November 15)
- Resources available (benefits portal, FAQ, HR contact)
- Virtual benefits fair invitation
- Encouraging closing that reduces anxiety

TONE & STYLE:
- Professional yet warm and approachable
- Action-oriented without being pushy
- Positive framing (emphasize improvements and new options)
- Anxiety-reducing (acknowledge this can be confusing, we're here to help)
- Consistent with Creighton's mission-driven, supportive culture

FORMAT:
- Email length: 200-300 words (scannable, not overwhelming)
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Include bullet points for key dates and resources
- Bold important dates and action items
- Consider using a "Quick Summary" box at the top
- Include clear call-to-action buttons/links (if HTML email)

AVOID:
- Benefits jargon without explanation
- Overwhelming detail (save for FAQ)
- Negative framing ("Don't miss out" vs. "Take advantage of")
- Making it sound like homework

BONUS ELEMENTS TO CONSIDER:
- Quick summary box with "Need to Know" highlights
- Personal story or testimonial about mental health benefit
- FAQ teaser ("Top 3 Questions We're Getting")
- Reminder about upcoming benefits webinar
```

## Common Variations

### Variation 1: Mid-Enrollment Reminder
```
Change purpose to: "Remind employees who haven't enrolled and address 
common questions we're receiving"

Add context: "As of November 8, 35% of employees have completed enrollment"

Update tone: "More urgent but still supportive"
```

### Variation 2: Policy Change Announcement
```
Change purpose to: "Communicate significant policy change and manage concerns"

Add context: "Remote work policy changing from unlimited to hybrid (3 days/week)"

Update tone: "Empathetic, change management focus, address anticipated concerns"
```

### Variation 3: Crisis Communication
```
Change purpose to: "Communicate urgent situation and provide reassurance"

Add context: "Campus safety incident, no ongoing threat, counseling available"

Update tone: "Calm, factual, supportive, clear on resources"
```
