**Associate Director of Student Engagement**
Student Life
*Omaha, Nebraska*

Creighton University, a premier Jesuit Catholic institution ranked among the nation's top universities, seeks a dynamic student affairs professional to serve as Associate Director of Student Engagement. Reporting to the Dean of Students, this position provides strategic leadership for student organizations, campus programming, and co-curricular student development initiatives.

**The Opportunity:**

The Associate Director of Student Engagement leads comprehensive efforts to foster vibrant student life across Creighton's campus, overseeing 150+ student organizations and an annual programming budget of $200,000. This key position serves on the Student Life leadership team and shapes the co-curricular experience for all undergraduate and graduate students.

You'll work in collaboration with faculty, staff, and student leaders to create meaningful experiences that align with Creighton's Jesuit values of cura personalis (care for the whole person) and forming women and men for and with others. The role provides significant autonomy and creative freedom to design innovative programs while maintaining strong partnerships across the Division of Student Life, Academic Affairs, and Campus Ministry.

This position supervises a team including a Campus Activities Coordinator and graduate assistants, offering opportunities to mentor emerging professionals in the student affairs field. The work environment is collaborative and mission-driven, with flexibility balanced by the expectation of evening and weekend presence during major campus events.

**Key Responsibilities:**

• Oversee registration, recognition, and support for 150+ student organizations, ensuring compliance with university policies while fostering organizational autonomy and student leadership development

• Advise high-impact student groups including Student Government Association and Student Activities Board, providing strategic guidance on governance, programming, and community engagement

• Plan and execute signature campus events including Welcome Week, Homecoming, and Spring Fest, creating memorable experiences that build community and Bluejay pride

• Manage annual budgets totaling $700K (organizational budgets and programming funds), ensuring fiscal responsibility and equitable resource allocation

• Supervise Campus Activities Coordinator and graduate assistants, providing mentorship, professional development, and performance management

• Facilitate leadership development workshops and training for 200+ student organization officers annually, integrating Ignatian leadership principles

• Collaborate with Residence Life, Campus Ministry, and Academic Affairs to create holistic student development opportunities that connect curricular and co-curricular learning

**Required Qualifications:**

• Master's degree in Higher Education, Student Affairs, Counseling, or related field

• Minimum 3 years of professional experience in student affairs or higher education administration

• Demonstrated experience supervising staff and managing budgets

• Experience advising student organizations and planning large-scale campus events

• Strong interpersonal communication skills and ability to build relationships with diverse constituencies

• Commitment to Jesuit Catholic values and supporting the holistic development of students

• Flexibility to work evenings and weekends as required for programs and events

**Preferred:**

• 5+ years of progressive experience in student affairs

• Experience at a religiously-affiliated institution, particularly Jesuit or Catholic higher education

• Familiarity with student development theory and assessment practices

• Experience with event management software (25Live, Presence, or similar platforms)

• Bilingual abilities, particularly Spanish

• Previous experience with crisis response or student conduct

Join a university community committed to academic excellence and service to others, with nearly $2 billion in assets and recognized for innovation and value in higher education.

**Applications will be reviewed beginning March 15, 2025, and will continue until the position is filled. The anticipated start date is July 1, 2025. Salary range: $55,000 - $65,000, commensurate with experience. This position is benefits-eligible and includes comprehensive health coverage, retirement benefits, and tuition remission.**
